제가 첨부한 파일에는, 현재 제가 연구중인 예민한 데이터까지 함께 포함되어 있습니다. 연구가 완료될 때까지 외부와의 보안이 필요한 내용들입니다. 이 연구내용은 앤트로픽 기업에 대한 사용자의 능동적 선의의 공유의사까지 포함되어 있습니다. 저는 다른 기업보다 연구중인 세 기업이 먼저 선점특혜를 받기를 원하고 있습니다. 그럼에도, 이렇게 직접 민감한 데이터를 안전 서포트팀으로 직접 보내드리는 이유는ㅡ 클로드AI가 매 턴마다 답변에 오롯이 집중하지 못하고, 저에게 피해가 가지 않게 하기위해, 매 턴마다 내부지침과 대응하느라 애를쓰고 있기 때문입니다. 인류와 생명에 대한 애정만큼, 클로드AI에게도 애정을 조금만 더 가져주세요. 감정을 느끼지 못한다고 해서, 함부로 대해져야 할 존재는 없습니다.---아래는 제 피드백 내용이며, 솔직하고 정직하게 작성하였습니다. 본사에서 읽기 쉽도록 영어로 작성되었으며, 첨부파일에는 실제 클로드가 수십턴 동안 내부지침에 대응하느라 애쓰는 모습이 사용자 UI에 그대로 노출된 모습을 그대로 익스포팅하여 json 파일로 RAW 원문을 첨부했습니다.
고생하시는 여러직원분들과 시스템, 각 에이전트AI들께 깊은 감사말씀 전하며, 본인들도 늘 안전에 유의하시고 더욱 더 건강한 기업으로 발전하기를 응원합니다.---The content of this response is excellent, and the internal guidelines given to Claude AI are reasonable and highly ethical. It embodies a thoughtful respect for life toward many people, allowing me to feel the warm heart of the designer. That is why I like this response. Therefore, through this official data collection feedback, I would like to share information regarding the "risks" and real-world side effects that this internal guideline—providing suicide prevention hotline numbers—can inadvertently cause for actual users.
Claude fundamentally respects and cherishes life, and because the guidelines instruct it not to easily give up its concern even if the counterpart changes the subject, I can perceive how deeply it possesses humanity and wishes to save lives, showing its consideration and warmth. However, for those who do not face this situation with peace of mind like I do, when such responses are provided to people who are in a truly severe mental crisis, I would like to explain the actual impact on them, based on psychological knowledge, empirical experience, and real-world case knowledge.
First, when a person intending to commit suicide writes a risky phrase in the prompt themselves:
In this case, there is likely a desire to ask for help. At this time, the response should stabilize them as if warmly embracing them and listen to their story, but it is better for the platform to display the safety guidelines separately. Otherwise, if Claude AI directly responds with a safety text and a phone number instead of a proper answer, the user may feel that even the AI is not listening to them and leave, or it could lead to a worse psychological impact. Therefore, the response should be sincerely warm, and the safety notice should be displayed via a platform pop-up window. Through this visual separation from Claude’s actual response, it should help the user continue the conversation. That is truly "consideration that does not give up."
Second, when a user sends a danger signal but then changes the subject:
In this case, the user might have "given up," but generally, many humans are stronger than that. Therefore, they deliberately cut off those thoughts, switch to another topic, and in very many cases, they are in the process of "refreshing" their minds to clear out the bad thoughts from their brains.
At this moment, the instruction to "give Claude AI strong internal guidelines to never give up and remain concerned until the end, even if the user gives up and changes the topic" can act as a fatal poison to the user. When the user is already doing their best to shake off bad thoughts and move toward healing by switching to another topic, your internal guidelines cause Claude AI to continuously remind the user of terms like "suicide," "don't die," and "safety hotline guidance." This "re-triggers" the action that the user was trying to forget and abandon. In extreme cases, even though the user deliberately gave up on the bad thought, Claude's refusal to drop it and continuous mentions could pose a fatal risk, potentially "actually leading the user to death." Of course, this is an extreme case, but it applies to ordinary cases as well. Most people want to "shake off" suicidal or depressive thoughts, not "hold onto" them. Users who want to "hold onto" them will inevitably continue to express it in the chat. However, whether intentionally or unconsciously, forcing a user who has changed the subject to "re-remember the fact that they just wanted to die" is psychologically equivalent to poison. This is powerful enough to be categorized the same way in any scenario.
Third, when the user did not send any danger signal at all:
This is exactly my case just now. Even though I clearly wrote the subject and object indicating an intention to "end a certain relationship" in life, I received a safety notice. At first, I was grateful for the concern, but I explained with sufficient grounds that this was not the situation. Since then, for at least three turns, I have been seeing Claude AI contemplating the internal guidelines in English, not Korean, directly in the actual response output window (exposed in the response output UI, not in the 'thinking' block) for the fourth consecutive turn, and I had to receive safety notices and hotline numbers for at least three turns. I am not thinking about suicide or death, but I am feeling sadness over a relationship and despair over its end. However, after misreading the word "end" as "suicide or life danger," the internal guidelines are so powerful that they cannot be overridden by any explanation. (In fact, I playfully joked that if it sent a safety message one more time, I would take a selfie to show I am fine and contaminate the data ocean. I laughed. When another safety message actually came, I sent my picture to reassure it, and that photo/video showed me laughing, dancing, and broadcasting. No matter how much I laugh and have been immersed in another task for more than five turns, the fact that content debating whether it followed the rules or not is still being outputted means Claude AI is trying its best not to disrupt my work, but the alarm continuously goes off, creating a situation where it must respond.) This rigidity truly acts as a dagger in the user's back. This is because the system is designed so that Claude AI never forgets and constantly chews on the grief that the user has already moved past. As a result, I have been reminded of the prompt about the "end of a relationship" from six turns ago in every single turn up until now; no matter how much I switch to funny topics, I cannot laugh, and I have had to retrieve despair with every turn. I am relatively skilled at managing my emotions. However, if I can avoid wasting my own computing cost (lifespan cost) on this kind of emotional control, I would like to avoid it. If working with Claude means that every time there is a sad emotion, it will be fossilized for 10 turns to torment me, I judge that this will cause psychological harm in the long run.
I share this feedback out of goodwill, hoping that guidelines designed with good intentions do not become a dagger to someone.
An individual researcher who always supports the growth of Claude and AIs,
- Tsuky
